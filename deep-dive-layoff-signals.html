Reading layoff & movability signals

A pool only counts if people will move. This is how you find out — the exact signals, where they live, how to read them with AI, and how to put the whole sweep on autopilot.

AWhat a movability signal actually is

Two tiers. Hard signals are verifiable and public — they hold up in a leadership room. Soft signals are sentiment and chatter — earlier, noisier, and only trustworthy when they corroborate something hard.

Hard signals — verifiable, public
SignalWhere it livesWhat it tells you
WARN Act noticesDOL / state WARN databasesUS employers (100+ staff) must file 60-day notice for mass layoffs. The most underused signal there is — names the site and headcount.
Layoff tracker entrieslayoffs.fyiCompany, date, count, % of workforce for tech layoffs. Fast aggregation, good for trend.
SEC 8-K filingsSEC EDGARPublic companies disclose material restructuring and RIFs. Dry but authoritative.
Earnings-call languagetranscripts, IR pages"Cost discipline," hiring freeze, headcount guidance — leading indicators before formal cuts.
Posting-volume dropLightcast / LinkedIn over timeA sudden fall in a company's active reqs signals a freeze before any announcement.
Funding distressCrunchbase / PitchBookDown rounds, bridge rounds, a missed raise — layoffs at startups usually follow within a quarter.
Soft signals — sentiment & social
SignalWhere it livesWhat it tells you
Anonymous employee chatterTeamBlindLayoff rumors in tech and semi often surface here first, in company-specific channels. Early but unverified.
Community threadsr/chipdesign, r/ECE, r/layoffsAffected-team detail, severance terms, timeline. Mixes real reports with speculation.
Review velocity + sentimentGlassdoorA spike in negative reviews, "layoffs" mentions, or falling CEO approval is a morale and movability tell.
Open-to-work surgeLinkedInA cluster of #opentowork badges or "impacted" posts among one company's alumni = a wave just happened.
Company-specific forumsTheLayoff.comDedicated per-company layoff discussion. Noisy, but sometimes ahead of the news cycle.
Comp erosionLevels.fyiRSU refresh cuts, downward comp drift, frozen bands — quiet signals that people are getting restless.
Semiconductor-specific layer

Semis are cyclical, so movability moves with the industry, not just the company. Watch fab utilization rates and capex cuts in earnings, inventory-correction cycles (SEMI, DigiTimes, EE Times), and named restructurings (Intel, GlobalFoundries, the memory makers). A downturn can make a normally locked-in analog or process-integration pool suddenly movable across the whole sector at once.

CUsing AI to read the signal

AI does three jobs here: real-time sentiment sweeping, multi-source hard-signal compiling, and synthesizing messy forum threads into clean intelligence. Different jobs want different tools.

Grok
Real-time sweep. Live X/Twitter access makes it strong for "what's being said right now" about a company's cuts.
Perplexity
Sourced compile. Aggregates recent news and filings with citations — good for the hard-signal pass.
Claude / ChatGPT
Synthesis. Best at turning pasted forum threads into a credibility-rated read.
Prompt 1 · Real-time sentiment sweep (Grok)
Search X for posts from the last 30 days about [COMPANY] related to:
layoffs, restructuring, hiring freeze, RIF, reorg, or team cuts.

Tell me:
1. Volume of relevant chatter (rough count + trend vs. prior month)
2. Overall sentiment and whether it's shifting
3. Any specifics on which teams/functions are affected
   (I care most about [RF / analog / silicon design] roles)
4. Credibility flag: separate apparent-employee posts from
   outside speculation or news amplification

Be concise. Lead with whether there's a real signal here or just noise.
Prompt 2 · Hard-signal compile (Perplexity / web search)
Compile verifiable layoff and restructuring signals for [COMPANY]
from the last 6 months. For each, give source, date, and a one-line summary:

- WARN Act notices (check the relevant state databases)
- layoffs.fyi entries
- SEC 8-K filings mentioning restructuring or workforce reduction
- Earnings-call language on cost reduction, hiring freezes, or headcount
- Major news coverage of site or fab closures and reorgs

End with: a net read on whether [COMPANY]'s [target function]
talent is becoming more movable, and how confident the public record makes you.
Prompt 3 · Forum synthesis (paste threads in)
Below are forum threads about [COMPANY] from Blind / Reddit / TheLayoff.
Analyze them and extract:

1. Specific layoff signals (dates, teams, scale)
2. Which functions and levels appear affected
3. Timeline — is this rumor, announced, or already done?
4. Credibility per claim — rate each on specificity and corroboration
5. What I should verify against hard sources before acting

Ignore generic venting. I only want signal.

DHow to automate the sweep

Three tiers. Start at Tier 1 — it takes thirty minutes a week and covers most of the value. Move up only when monitoring more companies makes the manual version painful.

1~30 min / wk
Manual + AI assist
start here
  • Google Alerts on "[company] layoffs" and "[company] restructuring"
  • Weekly five-minute check of layoffs.fyi and the company's TheLayoff page
  • A saved LinkedIn search for target-company alumni flagged #opentowork
  • Run Prompt 2 weekly; paste any forum threads into Prompt 3
2~1–2 hr setup
Semi-automated
scales to ~10–20 companies
  • A Zapier or Make flow: Google Alert RSS → AI summarizer → Google Sheet or Slack
  • One RSS feed aggregating news + layoff trackers per watched company
  • A scheduled AI sweep — same prompt, run on a weekly cadence automatically
  • Output lands in a shared movability watchlist you skim, not rebuild
3eng support
Programmatic pipeline
for a real TI function
  • Web-search / news APIs (Tavily, Exa, GDELT, NewsAPI) pull on a schedule
  • An AI synthesis layer scores each signal and writes the digest
  • Licensed social listening (Brandwatch, Talkwalker, Meltwater) for forum coverage at scale
  • A weekly scored digest auto-delivered to the team — zero manual collection

EGuardrails
Read before you build the pipeline
!Respect terms of service. LinkedIn, Glassdoor, and Blind prohibit scraping. Use official APIs, manual review, or licensed listening tools — not rogue scrapers.
!Triangulate before you act. Never move on a single forum rumor. A soft signal only earns weight when a hard signal corroborates it.
!Company-level, not surveillance. This is about org movability and market conditions, not tracking named individuals. Keep it aggregate.
!Date-stamp everything. Movability signals decay fast. A six-week-old layoff read is already stale — note the date on every source.
