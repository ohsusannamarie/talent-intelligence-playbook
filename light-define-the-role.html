Define the real role

Every wrong number downstream traces back to here. Size the wrong role and the pool, the ratio, and the comp benchmark are all confidently, precisely wrong. Get this right and the rest of the snapshot has a chance.

Light module · the fast version
AThe title is not the role

A req title is a label chosen for posting and leveling. It rarely describes the actual person you need to find — and in semis especially, the same title hides wildly different jobs.

What the req says

"Senior Hardware Engineer"

Sized against this, you'd scope a pool of tens of thousands — and brief the hiring manager on a market that has nothing to do with the actual hire.

What they actually need

RFIC designer, 28GHz+ mmWave, tape-out experience

The real pool is a few hundred people, concentrated in a handful of metros, and contested by three named rivals. A different snapshot entirely.

The gap between those two is the whole game. Closing it is what Step 1 is for.

BThe questions that surface it

You don't need a long intake. Four questions collapse a vague title into a definition you can actually search against.

1
What will they build in the first 6 months?
Cuts through the title to the concrete work. "Lead the 3nm PLL block" tells you the skill, the seniority, and the search far better than any level label.
2
Which 2-3 skills are non-negotiable?
Separates the must-haves from the wish list. The non-negotiables define the pool — everything else just ranks within it.
3
Who does this person look like today?
Ask for 1-2 real example profiles, current company and title. A named exemplar is worth a page of requirements — you reverse-engineer the search from it.
4
What's genuinely flexible?
Title, level, exact domain, location. Knowing what can flex tells you how wide the real pool is — and where to expand if the first scope comes back thin.
CUsing AI to pin it down

Drop in the JD and let AI strip it to a searchable definition — separating the real signal from boilerplate before you size anything.

Best tools: Claude or ChatGPT for the parse — they're strong at pulling structure out of a messy req.

Prompt · JD to searchable definition
Here is a job description: [paste JD].

Strip it to what I actually need to source against:
1. The 2-3 non-negotiable skills (ignore the wish list)
2. The real seniority, based on the work described — not the title
3. The specific domain / technology focus
4. 2-3 example current titles + companies a person like this works at today
5. What looks flexible vs. genuinely required

Flag anything in the JD that's boilerplate and should be ignored
when sizing the pool.
DGuardrails
Before you size anything
!Confirm the definition with the hiring manager. One message: "Sizing for an RFIC designer with mmWave tape-out — right?" Thirty seconds now saves a wrong snapshot later.
!Don't let the wish list define the pool. If you size against every "nice to have," you'll scope a pool of zero. Non-negotiables only.
!The exemplar profile is the fastest unlock. When the definition stays fuzzy, one real example LinkedIn profile resolves it faster than another round of questions.
